Title: St Helena

Lachlan Baldwin, Amaidi

In 1867 I was sent away to prison,
For the murder of a man who killed my son.
The Gaol at Petrie terrace was over loaded so they sent us,
To the island as a slave beneath the sun.

The first thing we did there cut the trees down to the clear,
Nowhere to hide and nowhere left to run.
After wrapping him in fabric, We dug a grave for old Fitzpatrick,
He was the first but not the last to succumb.

**These are our days on St Helena
And the waves crash on the shore so small
And far across the bay we can see the lights of Brisbane
So far away and yet not far at all**

Hard labour there was tough, breaks a man right to his core,
The sun hurt but the whip hurt even more.
We churned out bricks in the factory that built your city hall,
And at night I just stare blankly at the wall.

**These are our days on St Helena
And the waves crash on the shore so small
And far across the bay we can see the lights of Brisbane
So far away and yet not far at all**

One night old John McCauley,
Hatched a plan to for us to join him.
To escape back to the mainland Wynnum way.
With nothing left to beat us we climbed aboard the raft of timbers,
Setting sail for life beyond Morton bay.

But the power of the sea overwhelmed us gang of thieves,
And the frigid ocean water took its toll.
Old Moreton left her mark and fed our bodies to the sharks,
And lord had put to rest my suffering soul.

**These are our days on St Helena
And the waves crash on the shore so small
And far across the bay we can see the lights of Brisbane
So far away and yet not far at all**
