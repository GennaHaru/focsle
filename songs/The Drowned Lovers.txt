Title: The Drowned Lovers

As I was a-walking down by the sea shore
Where the wind whistled high and the waters did roar,
Where the wind whistled high and the waves raged around,
There I heard a fair maid make a pitiful sound

**Crying, ”Oh, my love is drowned,
My love must I deplore,
And I never, no never,
Shall see my love more.”**

I never a nobler, nor truer did see,
A lion encouraged, but gentle to me,
With an eye like an eagle, and a heart like a dove
And the songs that he sang me, were ever of love.

**Crying, ”Oh, my love is drowned,
My love must I deplore,
And I never, no never,
Shall see my love more.”**

The loss of my sailor I deeply deplore,
He is gone in the ocean, I'll see him no more,
Oh the shells of the oysters shall be my love's bed
And the fish in the sea shall swim over his head

**Crying, ”Oh, my love is drowned,
My love must I deplore,
And I never, no never,
Shall see my love more.”**

He is sunk in the ocean, he lies there asleep,
I will jump in beside him, I'll kiss his cold feet,
I will kiss the pale lips, once a coral like red,
And lie there beside him, for my True Love is dead.

**Crying, ”Oh, my love is drowned,
My love must I deplore,
And I never, no never,
Shall see my love more.”**
