Title: Street Cries

The Teacups

Turkey rhubarb, Turkey rhubarb, Turkey rhubarb I sell
I come here from Turkey to make you all well
Don't you all know me, for my name it is Dan
And I am the celebrated Turkey rhubarb man

**Turkey rhubarb, Turkey rhubarb, Turkey rhubarb I sell
I come here from Turkey to make you all well
Don't you all know me, for my name it is Dan
And I am the celebrated Turkey rhubarb man**

Young gentlemen, young gentlemen, attend to my cry
Put your hands in your pockets, and bring forth your knives
You know the barber's fine sharp razor, I take it for to grind
And sharpen all the scissors for you maidens and you wives

**Young gentlemen, young gentlemen, attend to my cry
Put your hands in your pockets, and bring forth your knives
You know the barber's fine sharp razor, I take it for to grind
And sharpen all the scissors for you maidens and you wives**

My mop is so big it might serve as a wig
For a judge, if he had no objections
And as for my brooms, they'll sweep dirty rooms
And they'll make the dust fly to perfection

**My mop is so big it might serve as a wig
For a judge, if he had no objections
And as for my brooms, they'll sweep dirty rooms
And they'll make the dust fly to perfection**

Let fame puff a trumpet for muffin and crumpet
They cannot compare with my dainty hot rolls
When mornings are chilly, sweet Fanny, young Billy
Your hearts they will comfort, my gay little souls

**Let fame puff a trumpet for muffin and crumpet
They cannot compare with my dainty hot rolls
When mornings are chilly, sweet Fanny, young Billy
Your hearts they will comfort, my gay little souls**

**All above verses at the same time twice**
